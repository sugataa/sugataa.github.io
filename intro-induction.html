Introduction to Induction

Fri 15 November 2013

Induction is a very familiar concept for a CS student, due to it being stressed in almost every introductory theoretical CS course. I'd like to cover it again in some detail.

I like to think of induction as the foundation for the theoretical side of computer science. It can be both rigorous and simple, depending on how you approach a problem.

Here are some of my most common steps in a proof by induction are:

1. Constructing a hypothesis.
2. Making an initial test.
3. Coming up with the lemma.
4. Following through with a inductive step.
5. Concluding the main idea.

It is often up to the individual writing the proof to strengthen each of these steps. There are various types of induction as well, such as simple (or mathematical), complete and structural.

I learn best by doing examples so let's work through one:
